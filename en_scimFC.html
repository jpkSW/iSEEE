SCIM/FC: Squirrel-Cage Induction Machine with Field-oriented Control

The two currents Id (green) and Iq (blue) in the inverse Γ-ECD have a phase shift of exactly 90°. (Why?) Therefore, it makes sense to interpret the direct current Id as "field-forming" and the transverse current Iq as "torque-forming".

Similar to the speed adjustment of the DC machine, the direct current will be kept constant as long as the stator voltage allows it. The flux linkage LmId generated with the direct current is kept constant analogous to the excitation flux of the DC machines.

A field weakening is realized here analogous to the field weakening of the DC machine, in that the direct current Id is reduced inversely proportional to the speed like the excitation current of the DC machine. However, in order to be able to generate the required torque nevertheless, the quadrature current Iq must be increased accordingly.

Depending on the load- and the speed-dependent voltage and frequency, the current locus (CL) changes. If the stator frequency fS increases faster than the stator voltage US, the CL becomes smaller and the current phasor IS (red) "moves" to the pull-out point.

In the case of field weakening, the direct current Id is reduced here in inverse proportion to the speed, like the excitation current If of the DC machine. In the case of the DC machine, this keeps the induced voltage Ui constant.

Here, the "induced" voltage ωSLmId is also kept constant by this method, but the voltage drop ωSLσIS naturally increases with the stator frequency. A comparable phenomenon does not exist for the DC machine.
